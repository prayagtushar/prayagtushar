# Behavioral — US/Global, junior edition

US behavioral rounds are structured and scored (Meta: a dedicated 45-minute round on
conflict, ambiguity, and drive; Amazon: Leadership Principles in *every* round). At 1 YOE
they don't expect leadership epics — they expect **ownership, learning velocity, and
quantified impact** from the experience you do have.

## The framework
STAR — Situation (1–2 lines), Task (your responsibility), Action (what *you* did; say "I"),
Result (a number: latency cut, cost saved, users served, tickets deflected). Then one line
of "what I learned/changed". Rambling context is the #1 junior failure mode.

## Prepare these 6 stories (cover ~95% of questions)

1. **Most rewarding/unique project** *(reported real question)* — expect follow-ups: why
   that one, what impact, **how did you measure it**. Have the metric ready.
2. **Technical challenge you fought through** — e.g. RAG retrieval quality: describe the
   debugging method, not just the fix.
3. **Disagreement with a colleague/senior** — resolved with data/prototype; you can commit
   to decisions you disagreed with.
4. **Deadline/ambiguity under pressure** *(reported: "How do you work under strict
   deadlines?")* — show prioritization + proactive communication, not heroics.
5. **A real mistake** — what broke, your part in it, the fix, the process change. Genuine
   beats polished.
6. **Improving the team beyond your ticket** *(reported: "As an IC, how would you improve
   team culture?")* — docs you wrote, a flaky test you fixed, onboarding help.

## Junior-specific questions to expect
- "Why AI engineering?" — tie to what you've *built*, not hype.
- "Tell me about a time you had to learn something quickly" — you're being hired for slope,
  not intercept; have a concrete 2-week-ramp story.
- "How do you handle feedback?" — include a real example of acting on critical feedback.
- Recruiter-screen basics: start date, visa/work authorization, location, salary
  expectations (US: you can ask for their range first; many states require posting it).

## Signals that score well
Quantify everything; own failures without blaming; show you sought feedback; end stories
with what changed in how you work. For Amazon specifically, label stories mentally by LP
(Ownership, Dive Deep, Bias for Action, Deliver Results) — interviewers literally file them
that way.
