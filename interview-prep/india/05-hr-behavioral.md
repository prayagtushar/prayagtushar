# HR / Behavioral Round — India, ~1 YOE

The HR round is rarely a filter, but a bad answer to "why are you leaving after one year?"
can sink an otherwise-cleared loop, and everything you say about CTC/notice period is
negotiation. Prepare these cold.

---

## The questions you WILL get

**1. Tell me about yourself.**
60–90 seconds: current role + stack → one quantified achievement → why this role. E.g.:
"I'm a backend/AI engineer with a year of experience building GenAI products — most recently
a RAG-based document-chat app using LangChain, Gemini and Pinecone serving real users. I own
features end-to-end from API design in NestJS to the retrieval pipeline. I'm looking for a
team where AI is the core product, which is why this role caught my eye."

**2. Why are you switching within a year?** *(the 1-YOE landmine)*
Never badmouth. Frame as running toward, not away: "I've learned a lot shipping X, but the
AI work there is a side project, not the product. I want my next years compounding in a team
that does this at production scale — that's exactly this role." If layoffs/instability are
the real reason, one neutral sentence of fact is fine.

**3. Why our company?**
Have 2 specifics: a product/tech-blog detail + how your skills map. Generic "great culture"
answers are noticed.

**4. Current CTC / expected CTC?**
Deflect expected as long as possible: "I'm sure your band for this role is fair — I'm more
interested in fit; what range has been budgeted?" If forced, give a range whose *bottom* you
would actually accept, anchored ~30–40% above current (typical 1-YOE switch hikes are
30–50%; more if underpaid). Never state a number lower than you want and then negotiate up.

**5. Notice period?**
Answer honestly (offers get rescinded for surprises). If it's 60–90 days, mention buyout
willingness only if you mean it.

**6. Are you open to relocation / hybrid / office?**
Decide before the call. A "no" late in the process wastes everyone's time; a fake "yes"
wastes yours.

**7. Do you have other offers?**
If yes, say so calmly without ultimatums — it speeds things up. If no: "I'm in process with
a couple of companies but this role is my priority."

---

## Behavioral (STAR) — prepare 5 stories from your 1 year

Structure every story: **S**ituation (1 line) → **T**ask (your responsibility) →
**A**ction (what *you* did — "I", not "we") → **R**esult (quantified).

Have one story each for:
1. **Hardest technical problem** — e.g. debugging retrieval quality in production RAG.
2. **Conflict/disagreement** — a design disagreement resolved with data or a prototype.
3. **Deadline pressure** — what you cut, how you communicated, what shipped.
4. **A mistake** — own it, show the fix AND the process change. Never "my weakness is
   perfectionism."
5. **Initiative beyond your ticket** — the side improvement nobody asked for that helped.

Reported real questions at junior level: "What was your most unique and rewarding project?"
(follow-ups: why, what impact, how did you measure it — have numbers), "How do you work
under strict deadlines?", "How would you improve team culture as an IC?"

---

## Questions YOU should ask

- "What does the first 90 days look like for this role?"
- "How does the team evaluate LLM features before shipping?" *(signals maturity)*
- "What's the growth path from this level?"

## Red flags to listen for
Vague answers about the team you'd join, "we'll decide the project after you join" (bench
risk at services companies), and pressure to accept same-day.
